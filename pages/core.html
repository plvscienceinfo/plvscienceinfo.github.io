The Layers

Crust
Forms the continents and ocean floor.
Made up of:
Granitemore_vert
Graniteclose

Formed from magma rich in silicon and oxygen. Makes up the upper part of the crust.

Basaltmore_vert
Basaltclose

Formed from magma rich in silicon and magnesium. Makes up the lower part of the crust.

5-10 km thick on oceanic parts. 30-50 km thick on continental parts.
Mantle
2,900 km thick.
Made up of solid rocks that may flow because of extreme heat and pressure.
Makes up 84% of the Earth's total volume.
Core
Divided into two layers, the outer layer which behaves like a liquid, and the inner layer which behaves like solid.
Made up of:
Iron
Nickel
Outer layer is 2,260 km thick, while the inner layer is 1,216 km thick.
